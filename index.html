18811 Colima Road is a fully-designed, density-bonus-approved 105-unit apartment community on a 1.07-acre corner parcel in Rowland Heights, San Gabriel Valley. The project carries an approved Administrative Housing Permit (RPPL2023003872) with all density-bonus waivers granted, and a complete architectural, structural, civil, and MEP construction-document set. The offering delivers a buyer a shovel-ready (RTI) entitlement — the entire design and entitlement runway is already retired.

The plan stacks four stories of Type V-A wood-frame residential over a two-level Type I-A concrete parking podium, yielding 84 density-bonus apartments plus 21 ADUs for 105 total income units, 144 parking stalls, a Level-2 podium courtyard, clubhouse, gym, and dog run. A new owner steps directly into permit issuance — the seller estimates remaining permits & fees of $1,167,190 to pull the building permit.

Location

The site occupies the hard corner of Colima Road and Paso Real Avenue in Rowland Heights, an established, high-density, supply-constrained rental submarket in the eastern San Gabriel Valley (unincorporated Los Angeles County). The location offers strong arterial visibility, immediate access to the SR-60 (Pomona) Freeway, and proximity to the Rowland Heights / Hacienda Heights retail, dining, and employment corridors. Surrounding uses are a mix of neighborhood commercial and residential, with the project’s commercial zoning and Community Standards District framework supporting the approved multifamily density.

The approved program delivers 105 income-producing dwelling units: 84 density-bonus apartments (57 one-bedroom and 27 two-bedroom homes) plus 21 accessory dwelling units (ADUs). State density-bonus law and the ADU statute together push effective density well beyond the base entitlement.

Valuation Framework

Approved, near-RTI multifamily development land is valued primarily on the entitlement it delivers — benchmarked on a per-unit, per-land-SF, and per-buildable-SF basis against comparable entitled / RTI apartment-site trades, then cross-checked against a residual analysis of stabilized value net of remaining cost to build.
